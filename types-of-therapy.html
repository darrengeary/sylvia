Types of Therapy

"Know all the theories, master all the techniques, but as you touch a human soul be just another human soul."
C.G. Jung
  • Cognitive Behaviour Therapy (CBT)
  • Psychodynamic Therapy
  • Reality Therapy
  • Solution Focused Brief Therapy

Cognitive Behaviour Therapy (CBT)

Explores thought patterns which in turn affects how you feel and ultimately how you behave. For example;

  • You were unsuccessful in your interview for a new job
  • You think you are not employable (thought)
  • You feel hopeless (feel)
  • You stop looking for possible work opportunities (behave)

If however you were to look at it from a different perspective

  • You think how do I improve my prospects (thought)
  • You begin to feel in control and hopeful (feel)
  • You enrol on a course that upskills you (behave)

CBT can give fresh insights and tools to view situations in a more positive way, in particular if negative thinking is our predominant pattern.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Explores your early childhood attachments to parents / caregivers and how these relationships are impacting on your life today. This is often unconscious / not in our awareness.

Exploration in therapy helps you to become aware that your thinking, feelings and behaviours are frequently still linked to childhood, you can then make conscious choices to update these outdated scripts and live a more positive life. For example:

  • As a child you only felt loved and accepted when you were obedient
  • Now as an adult, you constantly try to people please even to your own detriment so others will find you acceptable.
  • Therapy can help identify these patterns and you can choose new ways of being.

Reality Therapy

Explores how to look at your problem and identify what would be the best solution, therefore focus on what steps you can take to achieve same. For example:

  • Your mother refuses to accept that you are now an adult and capable to make your own life choices.
  • This is causing conflict in your relationship.
  • Discuss what you both need/expect from what is now an adult relationship and the boundaries necessary to maintain a healthier relationship.

Solution Focused Brief Therapy

Helps you identify and find ways to resolve a short term situation that is causing you concern. For example:

  • You are constantly overwhelmed with work commitments.
  • You can identify that your workload has doubled since the company secured new orders.
  • Address this with your boss, highlighting your desire to work to the best of your ability, however the new workload is compromising the quality of your work.
  • Explore the possibility of extra staff to reduce your stress and therefore maximise efficiency for new business.
